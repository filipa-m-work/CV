Why I'm moving into tech
I stopped asking "how?"
and started asking "why?"

For over a decade, I worked the way a lot of creative professionals do — project to project, client to client, always moving. I led restaurant teams in London. I directed photo and film productions across Portugal and the UK. I worked on projects ranging from BBC documentaries to Fast & Furious 10.

I became the person teams called when they needed someone who could translate between creative vision and operational reality.

But somewhere in the middle of juggling simultaneous projects across different time zones, I realised something: I was very good at starting things. I was less good at seeing them through to their full potential.

I've been the hospitality worker navigating the friction points of broken service workflows. The photographer with no idea if their content is performing. The tourist frustrated by a booking experience. I understand users because I've been them.

I want to stop being a hired gun and start being a builder. I want to go deep, work on a product I can care about, with a team I can grow with.

Let's build something that lasts.
